Definitions

Definitions

Use these definitions to align lesson requests with the intended activity difficulty and cybersecurity topic focus.

Activity Level

Beginner

Introduces foundational cybersecurity awareness concepts with no prior knowledge required. Focused on recognizing common online risks and learning basic safety practices. Students will typically interact with guided games, simple simulations, or hands-on demonstrations.

Intermediate

Builds on foundational knowledge and requires students to apply cybersecurity concepts in structured scenarios. Activities may involve solving guided challenges, analyzing information, or using basic cybersecurity tools.

Advanced

Requires technical engagement and independent problem-solving using cybersecurity concepts and tools. Activities often simulate real cybersecurity tasks such as solving Capture-The-Flag (CTF) challenges, using command-line tools, or breaking puzzles.

Learning Topic / Skill Area Definitions

Online Safety & Digital Citizenship

Responsible and respectful behavior when using the internet and digital technologies. This topic includes recognizing unsafe situations, understanding appropriate communication, and making safe decisions when interacting with websites, apps, and other users.

Privacy & Personal Data Protection

Understanding how personal information is collected, stored, shared, and protected online. Topics include personally identified information (PII), digital footprints, data tracking, privacy settings, how companies collect and use user data, and strategies for protecting PII.

Passwords & Authentication

Methods used to secure accounts and verify a user’s identity. Covers creating and managing strong passwords, password complexity, risks of password reuse, password managers, and common password attacks such as brute-force or dictionary attacks. Also includes authentication methods such as multi-factor authentication (MFA), biometrics, and security tokens.

Phishing & Social Engineering

Techniques used by attackers to manipulate people into revealing sensitive information or performing unsafe actions. This topic includes identifying phishing emails, fake websites, scams, and other deceptive messages, as well as understanding common social engineering tactics such as impersonation, urgency and pretexting.

Cryptography

Techniques used to protect information by converting it into secure formats that unauthorized users cannot easily read. Topics include encryption and decryption, ciphers, cryptographic keys, hashing, and how cryptography helps ensure confidentiality and integrity.

Malware & Cyber Threats

Types of malicious software and cyber attacks designed to harm systems, steal information, or disrupt operations and services. Includes malware such as viruses, worms, spyware, and ransomware, and other cyber threats such as denial-of-service attacks and malicious downloads.

Operating Systems & Command Line

Basic interaction with computer operating systems and system-level tools used to manage files, programs, and system resources. Topics include navigating file systems, executing commands in a terminal/command prompt, managing files and permissions, etc.

Network Security

Protecting data and systems as they travel across or connect within networks. Understanding how information moves between devices, common network-based threats such as packet interception, man-in-the-middle, denial-of-service attacks.

Artificial Intelligence

Understanding how artificial intelligence systems work and the security risks associated with them. Protecting artificial intelligence systems from manipulation, misuse, and adversarial attacks. Understanding how AI models are trained, deployed, and interacted with, as well as common AI-specific threats such as prompt injection and data poisoning.
